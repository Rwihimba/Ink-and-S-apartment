Kigali is famously clean, safe and easy to get around — but where you base yourself still shapes your whole trip. This guide breaks down the neighbourhoods travelers ask about most, so you can pick the right home for work, leisure or a bit of both.

## First, how Kigali is laid out

Kigali is a city of hills and ridges, and its districts sit along and between them. Journeys are short — most cross-town trips are 10–20 minutes — but traffic builds around the CBD at peak hours. The sweet spot for most visitors is a calm residential area that's still minutes from business and dining hubs. That's exactly why we're based in **Kacyiru**.

## Kacyiru & Gishushu

Leafy, quiet and central, Kacyiru is home to embassies, government offices and the kind of streets you can actually walk in the evening. It's minutes from the Kigali Convention Centre, Kigali Heights and BK Arena, and only about 12 minutes from the airport.

- **Best for:** business travelers, longer stays, anyone who wants quiet nights without being far from the action.
- **Nearby:** Simba Center (groceries), Kigali Heights (dining & cafés), the Convention Centre precinct.

## Kimihurura

Just next door, Kimihurura is Kigali's nightlife-and-restaurants belt: lounges, coffee shops and some of the city's best-loved eateries. It's lively in the evenings and still very central.

- **Best for:** foodies and social travelers who want restaurants on their doorstep.

## Nyarutarama & Kacyiru Heights

Green, upscale and residential, wrapped around the golf course and lake. Beautiful and calm, but a little further from the buzz.

- **Best for:** families and those prioritising space and quiet over walkability.

## City Centre (CBD)

The commercial heart — banks, offices and hotels. Convenient for back-to-back downtown meetings, but busier and noisier, and quieter after dark.

- **Best for:** short trips built entirely around the CBD.
