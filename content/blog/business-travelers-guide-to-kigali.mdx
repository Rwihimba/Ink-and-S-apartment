Kigali has quietly become one of Africa's easiest cities to do business in: safe, organised, well-connected and increasingly a hub for conferences and regional HQs. If you're heading in for work, here's what actually matters on the ground.

## Getting in and getting connected

- **Airport:** Kigali International (KGL) is compact and close to the city — around 12 minutes to Kacyiru. Immigration is quick, and a **visa on arrival** is available to many nationalities (check your specifics before you fly).
- **SIM & data:** Grab an MTN or Airtel SIM at the airport or in town; data is cheap and coverage is strong. **Mobile Money (MoMo)** is used everywhere — for taxis, shops and even paying your host.
- **Power & water:** Reliable, but pack a universal adapter (Type C/J). Serviced apartments with **backup power** keep you working through any outage.

## Getting around

- **Moto-taxis** are the fastest way across town — hail one or book via the Move or Yego Moto apps.
- **Ride-hailing:** Yego Cabs and VW Move give you metered, app-based cars for meetings and evenings out.
- Traffic is light by regional standards, but leave a buffer around the CBD at rush hour.

## Where to work

A good base matters more than a hotel lobby. Look for:

- **Fast fibre WiFi** that handles video calls without drama.
- **A real desk** and quiet, so you can focus between meetings.
- **A central-but-calm location** for short hops to the Convention Centre and Kigali Heights.

## Meetings, events & downtime

- The **Kigali Convention Centre** and **Kigali Heights** host most major conferences and have cafés and restaurants built in.
- **BK Arena** covers big events and shows.
- For downtime, the Kimihurura restaurant strip is minutes away, and a weekend trip to Nyungwe or Volcanoes National Park is very doable.

> Practical tip: book accommodation with **self check-in** so a late flight never means a locked door — and confirm **airport pickup** in advance so your first ride is sorted.
